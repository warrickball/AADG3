subroutine  math/randn(x)¶

Populates the array x with normally-distributed random variates with zero mean and unit variance using the polar form of the Box-Müller transform. The length of x must be even otherwise the subroutine will stop.

subroutine  math/lm_to_k(l, m, k)¶

A one-to-one relation that maps a pair of integers (l, m), with 0 <= m <= l, into a continuous sequence. Useful for looping over sequences of angular degrees and azimuthal orders,

This is the inverse of k_to_lm.

Parameters

  • l [integer,in]

  • m [integer,in]

  • k [integer,out]

Called from

aadg3, test_aadg3

subroutine  math/k_to_lm(k, l, m)¶

A one-to-one relation that maps a non-negative integer to pair of integers (l, m), with 0 <= m <= l, assuming zero maps to (0, 0). Useful for looping over sequences of angular degrees and azimuthal orders,

This is the inverse of lm_to_k.

Parameters

  • k [integer,in]

  • l [integer,out]

subroutine  core/generate_kicks(n_fine, tau_cad, nsd, kicks)¶

Generates an exponentially-damped random walk (i.e. an AR(1) process) that AADG3 uses to model granulation, for either the correlated or uncorrelated component of the driving term.
